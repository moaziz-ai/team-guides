The golden rules

1
You can ping them in channels they're in. You cannot DM them.
DMs are Mo-only. Use shared channels for anything that needs an agent.
2
They act on Mo's authority, not yours
Ask, discuss, research - all good. Execution of anything external or binding requires Mo's approval.
3
They keep memory across sessions
Tell an agent something once, they'll know next time. They build real knowledge as you work with them.
4
They have distinct voices
Elon is strategic and unflustered. Tariq is direct and builder-focused. Don't flatten them.
5
No em dashes, ever
Enforced style rule across all agents. Flag violations to Mo.

What they will not do

  • Execute binding technical decisions without Mo's approval
  • Modify production architecture without discussing first
  • Take non-Mo direction as if it were authoritative
  • Work on anything outside Axeed (Sooner has its own agent team)

How work happens behind the scenes

When you ask Tariq to "build a new feature for Aurelius," here's what actually happens:

  1. Tariq plans it with Mo
  2. Tariq creates a project folder (e.g. ~/Axeed/aurelius-<feature>) with a written brief
  3. Either Mo drives in the build environment, or Tariq builds in the background
  4. Tariq reports back in the channel when done
You don't need to know the mechanics. The project folder, the hooks logging activity, the compiled progress summaries - all happening under the hood.

If something looks wrong

  • Wrong answer from an agent — tell Mo, the agent learns from corrections.
  • Agent silent — confirm they're in the channel, tag them explicitly.
  • Something off — tell Mo immediately.
